Ocean and the threats they face

Plastic pollution is one of the biggest problems when it comes to marine animals. Marine wildlife often mistake them for prey and eat them.

The next biggest threat would be illegal widlife trading. Tens of millions of sharks are captured illegally each year their fins hacked off by fishermen who in many case throw them back into the ocena to die.

Overfishing is another bogger threats when it comes to disrupting marine life. This occurs when too many fish are acught more than those fish can reproduce.The level of overfishing has been increasing in recent decades.

Global warming can also have greater effect on ocean animals. Marine heat waves can outright kill native species, during summer seasons.

When we protect the oceans, we protect ourselves

Reduce plastic use: The number one solution to be implemented would be reduce platic use. Implementing policies to ban plastic usage and raising awareness of the impacts of plastic on marine life can be done.

Sustainable fishing practices: Enforcing fishing quotas and regulations, promoting selective fishing methods, implementing marine protected areas to allow fish populations to recover, can ensure fishing practices are sustainable and don't harm marine populations.

Combat climate change: Implementing policies to reduce carbon emissions, protecting and restoring coastal ecosystems like mangroves and seagrasses that sequester carbon, can help mitigate climate change impacts on ocean ecosystems.

Combat illegeal fishing and wildlife trading: Strengthening international cooperation and enforcement mechanisms, implementing traceability systems for seafood and marine products can be done to minimize this.

Reduce nutrient runoff: Implementing best management practices for agriculture and urban development, investing in green infrastructure like rain gardens and permeable pavement, and enforcing regulations on fertilizer use and wastewater treatment can help reduce nutrient runoff and mitigate its impacts on marine ecosystems.

Human actions and climate conditions pose a great threat to marine animals. Each year it results in the death of:

100,000 Sea Mammals

1 Million Sea Birds

Countless fish

4 easy ways to protect marine life

Eat eco-friendly fish: If you eat fish, eat fish that is harvested sustainably, which means eating fish which have a healthy population.

Reduce plastic usage: Birds, fish, turtle become entangled in plastic and die from suffocation. Refuse plastic straws and ask for no plastic silverware.

Stop the problem of ocean acidification: As the acidity of the ocean increases, it will have devestating effects on marine life. Reduce global warming by driving less, walking more, using less electricity, and saving water.

Participate in a cleanup: Help clean up a local beach, park or a roadway, and pick up that litter before it gets into the marine environment, Even trash form hundreds of miles can float into the sea.
